Premium Mobility Protocol
TUE + THU · 12–15 MIN
Built for the exact zones heavy rack pulls and screen time chew up: lower back, neck, hips, thoracic spine. Sequence runs dynamic → static, spine → hips → neck → down-regulation.
01
Cat-Cow Flow
3 × 10 SLOW REPS
Segmental spinal movement — decompresses the lower back and wakes up the whole spine before anything else.
02
World's Greatest Stretch
2 × 5 EACH SIDE · 3 SEC HOLD
Hip flexor + t-spine rotation + hamstring in one flow. Covers more ground than almost anything else on this list.
03
90/90 Hip Switch
2 × 8 EACH SIDE
Hip internal/external rotation. Directly supports glute activation and counters anterior pelvic tilt.
Priority if short on time
04
Child's Pose to Cobra
2 × 8 SLOW REPS · 2 SEC PAUSE
Lower back decompression paired with gentle spinal extension. Undoes compression from rack pulls and hip thrusts.
05
Thread the Needle
2 × 6 EACH SIDE · 3 SEC HOLD
Thoracic rotation. Opens the upper back and takes pressure off the neck — critical for screen-heavy days.
Priority if short on time
06
Neck CARs
2 × 5 SLOW CIRCLES EACH WAY
Controlled Articular Rotations — full range through flexion, extension, rotation. Active control, not passive stretching. What actually protects the neck long term.
07
Standing Forward Fold with Sway
1 × 45–60 SEC · GENTLE SWAY
Passive hamstring and lower back release. Let gravity do the work — no forcing it.
08
90-Degree Wall Hip Flexor Stretch
2 × 30 SEC EACH SIDE
Direct anterior pelvic tilt correction. Back foot elevated against a wall or bench, hips pressed forward.
Priority if short on time
09
Supine Figure-4 Stretch
2 × 30 SEC EACH SIDE
Piriformis and deep glute release — often the hidden contributor to lower back tightness that stretching the back alone won't fix.
10
Deep Breathing Reset
1 × 2 MIN · KNEES BENT
Diaphragmatic breathing — 4 sec in, 6 sec out. Down-regulates the nervous system and lets everything you just mobilized settle in.
Short on time? Moves 03, 05, and 08 alone cover your biggest structural weak points — anterior pelvic tilt, internally rotated shoulders, hip tightness from rack pulls — in under 5 minutes.

Operating Rules
NON-NEGOTIABLE
RULE 01
Double Progression — The Engine
Every exercise has a rep range. When you hit the top of the range on ALL sets with clean form, add weight next session. That's the entire system. Log every session — without the log, there is no progression, just exercise.
RULE 02
Effort: 1–2 Reps From Failure
You're in a surplus — recovery supports real effort. Every working set ends 1–2 clean reps shy of failure. If you could have done 4 more reps, the set didn't count.
RULE 03
Low Volume Is Intentional
Mostly 2 working sets per exercise. 2 sets done brutally hard with progression beats 4 sets of pacing. Do not add sets. Add weight, add reps, add intensity.
RULE 04
Recovery Days Have a Job
Tuesday and Thursday aren't "off" — they're Zone 2, conditioning, abs, and mobility with intent. Skipping them isn't extra rest, it's a missed opportunity to recover faster and move better.
RULE 05
Sunday Is Sacred
One day a week, nothing gets measured. No macros, no sets, no reps. Full nervous system reset — sun, water, ground.
RULE 06
Fatigue Checkpoint
If two consecutive sessions show strength regression on your logged lifts, cut volume 30% for 4–5 days, sleep more, then resume. Regression is data, not failure.
RULE 07
Zone 2 Capped
2–3 sessions per week maximum. Cardio is a health tool now, not a fat loss tool. Daily fasted cardio eats the surplus you're trying to build with.
RULE 08
Vacuums & Walk — Unchanged
3 rounds of vacuums + 20 min walk, every morning, fasted. Not part of any lifting session. These happen regardless of what day it is.
